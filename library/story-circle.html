The Story Circle

Fifteen minutes, any table, any language. A parable is not finished until it has been spoken aloud between people.

The rite

  1. The Asker reads the parable's opening question aloud, and everyone answers it from their own life — one minute each, youngest first.
  2. The Teller reads the parable — or better, retells it from the "Retell it in 60 seconds" skeleton on its page.
  3. A breath of silence.
  4. Everyone answers the two Ask each other questions at the bottom of the page. Disagreement is part of the rite; the story holds all of it.
  5. The Teller closes by reading the Line (the moral) once, slowly. Whoever it lands on hardest becomes next week's Teller.

The only rules

No phones on the table except the one holding the story. No explaining anyone else's answer. The youngest always answers first — the story belongs to them longest.

Print this page. A circle that meets weekly needs nothing else — pick each week's parable from the library, or use tonight's.
